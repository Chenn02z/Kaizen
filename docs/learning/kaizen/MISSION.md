# Mission: Learn AI Engineering by Building Kaizen

## Why
I am building Kaizen to learn practical AI engineering while shipping a real project I can defend in internship and full-time interviews. The goal is not just to make features work, but to understand the design, tradeoffs, and evaluation methods well enough to explain them clearly.

## Success looks like
- I can implement each Kaizen milestone and explain why the architecture, prompts, retrieval, memory, and eval choices exist.
- I can discuss RAG, structured extraction, memory, proactive agents, and observability using concrete examples from this repo.
- The repo contains tested, reviewable artifacts that support credible resume bullets and interview stories.

## Proof / portfolio outcomes
- Each milestone ends with working code, tests, and a short explanation of the important AI engineering decisions.
- I can point to concrete files, evals, and traces when describing what I built.
- I maintain learning records for non-obvious concepts and tradeoffs I now understand.

## Constraints
- The project needs to stay scoped enough to ship.
- Learning should happen alongside implementation, not as a detached curriculum.
- Explanations should stay grounded in this repo's code and docs first.

## Out of scope
- Broad survey-style AI theory that does not help Kaizen ship.
- Resume embellishment without real repo evidence.
- Chasing adjacent tooling unless it directly unblocks a milestone.
