# Urge Surfing

Urge surfing is a mindfulness-based technique for riding out cravings without acting on them. Rather than fighting the urge or distracting yourself, you observe it as a wave — it rises, peaks, and eventually falls on its own without you having to do anything.

The technique draws from acceptance-based behavioral therapy. Cravings are not commands; they are sensations that arise and pass. Most urges peak within fifteen to thirty minutes. When you resist the impulse to act immediately and simply observe the craving, you build evidence that you can tolerate discomfort, which weakens the craving's power over time.

When you feel the pull to skip your evening run, eat something you had planned to avoid, or break a no-social-media hour, try pausing for a moment. Notice where the urge lives in your body — tension in your chest, restlessness in your legs. Label it: "I am noticing an urge to skip." Watch it without judgment, as if you were studying a wave. Give it a few minutes. You will often find it loses intensity before the fifteen-minute mark.

You do not need to fight the urge or argue with yourself about it. The act of observing it — naming it, feeling it without reacting — is the technique. Over weeks, this builds the muscle of tolerating discomfort, which is central to changing any habit driven by short-term relief.
