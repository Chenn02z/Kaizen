# Urge Surfing

source: Atomic Habits and Tiny Habits

technique: urge surfing, craving awareness, prompt interruption

lesson: Urge surfing is not a central named tactic in either book, but it fits their habit loop logic: pause between cue and response long enough to choose a different action. Atomic Habits helps identify the cue and craving behind the unwanted behavior. Tiny Habits helps replace the response with a tiny alternative that is easy to do once the urge has been noticed.

use_when: Use this when the user reports cravings, boredom snacking, Instagram checking, impulsive skipping, or an urge that rises at a predictable time.

avoid_when: Avoid this when the behavior involves addiction risk, severe distress, or safety concerns. Then Kaizen should recommend human support rather than a simple habit tactic.

example_application: If the user craves chips at 3pm, pause for one minute, label the sensation as an urge, drink water, and do the tiny replacement action of walking to a window before deciding.
