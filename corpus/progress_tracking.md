# Progress Tracking

source: Atomic Habits and Tiny Habits

technique: progress tracking, feedback loops, celebration

lesson: Tracking turns invisible repetition into evidence. Atomic Habits emphasizes that visible progress can make a behavior satisfying and identity-reinforcing. Tiny Habits adds that the emotional signal matters: the user should notice and feel the win right after the behavior, even when the behavior is tiny. Track the smallest meaningful completion, not only the ideal outcome.

use_when: Use this when the user mentions streaks, partial completions, logging meals, steps, workouts, water, sleep, or wanting to see whether a pattern is improving.

avoid_when: Avoid heavy tracking when the user is perfectionistic, anxious, or likely to treat missed days as failure. In that case, track recovery or attempts instead of perfect outcomes.

example_application: If the user read one page instead of thirty, log the page as a successful reading vote and add a short note about what made starting possible.
