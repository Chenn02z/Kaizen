# Two-Minute Rule

source: Atomic Habits and Tiny Habits

technique: two-minute rule, tiny habits, starter step

lesson: The smallest version of a habit is not a trick; it is the doorway into consistency. Atomic Habits uses a two-minute starter to reduce the weight of beginning. Tiny Habits generalizes the same principle: make the behavior so small that ability is high even when motivation is low. The starter step should count as success by itself, while leaving room to continue when energy appears.

use_when: Use this when the user feels resistance, fatigue, perfectionism, or keeps skipping because the full behavior feels too big.

avoid_when: Avoid prescribing the two-minute version as a ceiling. The user can continue after the tiny action, but the tiny action is the promised minimum.

example_application: If the user avoids 30-minute meditation, the tiny version is sitting down after coffee and taking three deliberate breaths.
