# Temptation Bundling

Temptation bundling pairs a behavior you want to do but keep avoiding with something you genuinely enjoy, so the reward is only available when you engage in the target habit. You are essentially bribing yourself with immediate pleasure.

The psychology is grounded in the principle that behaviors with immediate rewards are much more likely to be repeated than those with delayed payoffs. By attaching an instant reward to a habit that has only long-term benefits, you shift the short-term cost-benefit calculation in favor of doing it.

A classic example: only allow yourself to listen to your favorite podcast or audiobook while you are at the gym or on a walk. The anticipation of the enjoyable content becomes a pull toward the gym rather than a reason to procrastinate. When you skip the workout, you also forfeit the entertainment — which makes the trade-off feel more concrete.

When you apply this to your own routines, choose a genuine indulgence that you look forward to and would not otherwise deprive yourself of. The pairing has to feel like a treat, not a punishment. Try: only watch your favorite TV show while doing meal prep, or only listen to certain music while going through your evening stretching routine.
