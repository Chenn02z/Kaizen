# Identity-Based Habits

source: Atomic Habits and Tiny Habits

technique: identity-based habits, evidence loops, celebration

lesson: Identity changes when the user collects repeated evidence that a new self-description is true. Atomic Habits gives the core frame: actions are votes for the type of person the user is becoming. Tiny Habits adds an important operational detail: immediately noticing success helps the brain encode the behavior as rewarding. The identity should be earned through small proofs, not declared as pressure.

use_when: Use this when the user reports a win, a streak, a partial success, or language like "I am becoming the kind of person who..." It is useful for reinforcing momentum after small actions.

avoid_when: Avoid this when the user is in a shame spiral. Identity language can backfire if it implies that a miss means they are the wrong kind of person.

example_application: If the user took a short walk despite being tired, frame it as evidence that they are someone who keeps a minimum promise to their body.
