# Habit Stacking

source: Atomic Habits and Tiny Habits

technique: habit stacking, anchor prompts, routine chaining

lesson: A reliable existing routine can become the prompt for a new behavior. Atomic Habits describes attaching a new action to a current habit; Tiny Habits calls the current routine an anchor. The distilled rule is to choose an anchor that happens at the same frequency and location as the target behavior, then make the new behavior small enough to fit immediately after it.

use_when: Use this when the user already has a stable routine such as coffee, brushing teeth, lunch, shutdown, or getting into bed, and wants to add a small behavior nearby.

avoid_when: Avoid stacking onto vague or inconsistent anchors. "After work" is weaker than "after I put my laptop on the charger" because the latter is observable.

example_application: If the user wants to take vitamins daily, place them beside the coffee beans and use grinding coffee as the anchor for taking one vitamin.
